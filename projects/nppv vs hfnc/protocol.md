**2.** **Research question**

---

**P (Population): Patients with acute respiratory failure** **with hypercapnia**

**I (Intervention): High** **flow nasal cannula** **(H**FNC**)**

**C (Comparison): Noninvasive positive pressure ventilation (**NiPPV**)**

**O (Outcome): Clinical eff**icacy **(e.g., avoidance of intubation, therapy failure, improvement in dyspnea or gas exchange, patient comfort)**

---

**3.Method**

**3.1 Protocol**

**We used a systematic review protocol** **template(**https://www.protocols.io/view/protocol-template-for-intervention-review-cft4tnqw). We followed the Preferred reporting items for systematic review and meta-analysis 2020 (PRISMA-2020) for preparing this **protoco**l****[**12]. We will publish this protocol in** **OSF** **(**https://osf.io**)**.** **

---

**3.2 Inclusion criteria of the articles for the review**

**3.2.1 Type of studies**

**We will include randomized controlled trials (RCTs**), and **cluster RCTs that assess the efficacy of** **HFNC** **as compared with** **NPPiV**. We will not apply language or country restrictions. We will include all papers including published, unpublished articles, **abstract** **of** **conference** **and letters.**

**We will exclude non**-**randomized studies of interventions such as observational studies. We will not exclude studies based on the observation period or publication year.**

---

** 3.2.2 Study participants**

---

**Inclusion criteria:**

**Adult patients older than 18 years with hypoxic respiratory failure with hypercapnia proven by** **(1) partial pressure of carbon dioxide (**pCO **2) equal or greater than 60 mm Hg on blood gas analysis or (2) serum pH between 7.0 and 7.35 on venous blood gas analys**is. **Studies enrolling mixed populations were eligible if** **the majority of** **participants met the prespecified population criteria (adult critically ill ICU patients receiving gastric enteral nutrition), or if outcome data for the eligible subgroup could be extracted separately.**

---

**Exclusion criteria:**

**The** **exclusion criteria** **were** **patients who (**1) **needs** **immediate endotracheal intubation, (2) presents with cardiac or pulmonary arrest**.** **

---

**3.2.3 Intervention**

**The intervention group was high-flow nasal cannula/high-flow nasal oxygen delivering heated and humidified air/oxygen mixture via a nasal interface, with adjustable** **FiO**₂ (e.g., 0.21–1.0) and delivery of high flow (as defined by the authors or by device capability**).**. ** **

---

**3.2.4 Control**

**T**he **control** **group** **was noninvasive positive pressure ventilation delivered via a noninvasive interface (e.g., face/nasal mask or helmet), including bilevel (variable positive airway pressure with higher inspiratory and lower expi**ratory pressures) and/or CPAP. We extracted ventilator mode, interface, and key settings (IPAP/EPAP or CPAP level, backup rate, **FiO**₂/oxygen targets, and treatment duration) a priori and explored heterogeneity using subgroup/sensitivity analyses.** **

---

**3.3 Type of outcomes**

**3.3.1 Primary outcomes**

**1. ** ** **

**Treatment failure**
Definition: Treatment failure rate is defined as the occurrence of either all-cause **death** **or unplanned endotracheal intubation within the pre-specified follow-up window. Each participant** **contributes** **one event at first occurrence of either** **component**.** **
Period: We will preferentially extract outcomes at 28–30 days. If unavailable, we will use, in order: in-hospital, ICU stay, 14 days, 90 days. We will document the chosen window per study and explore heterogeneity by time horizon.** **

---

**2**. **Perception** **of comfort**

**Definition**:  **Perception** **of comfort will be extracted as a continuous outcome, primarily based on visual analog scales (VAS) or other validated patient-reported instruments as reported in the primary studies. When multiple time points are reported, we will extract data at the** **closest follow up after 72 hours of initiation** **of assessment within each study, as this** **represents** **the most clinically relevant and consistently reported measure.** **If studies report comfort on the same scale, we will pool mean differences (MD); otherwise, we will pool standardized mean differences (SMD; Hedges’ g) after aligning scale direction (higher = greater comfort).**

**Period:** **until 72 hours**

---

**3**. Duration of mechanical ventilation** **

**Definition: Time from randomization until first successful unassisted breathing or death. Mean or median time (hours) until unassisted breathing, defined as breathing without inspiratory support, extracorporeal lung support, or noninvasive ventilation delivering volume/pressure support. Continuous positive airway pressure and high-flow oxygen are not considered assisted ventilation. Success** **requires** **≥48 hours of unassisted breathing.** **Follow-up** **to 60 days from randomization. Continuous** **outcome;** **pooled using MD or SMD, with survivors and** **non-survivors** **analyzed separately if available.**

**Period: The longest follow up**

---

---

**4**. Length of stay (LOS)** **

**Definition: Time from randomization until the patient is first discharged from the relevant facility (ICU and/or hospital) or death. Mean or median time (hours or days) until discharge.** **Follow-up** **up to 60 days from randomization. ICU and hospital LOS will be extracted separately where reported. Investigators’ definitions of ICU and hospital LOS (e.g., open vs. closed ICU, national healthcare context) will be recorded. Continuous** **outcome;** **MD or SMD, analyzed separately for ICU and hospital LOS.**

**Period: The longest follow up**

---

**5**. Health-related quality of life (HRQOL)** **

**Definition: Patient-reported quality of life following randomization.** **Assessed using the EQ-5D-5L instrument at 6 months post-randomization. Reported as distribution of responses for each dimension (mobility, self-care, usual activities, pain/discomfort, anxiety/depression) and as mean visual analog scale (VAS) score (0–100). If alternative validated tools are used (e.g., SF-36), these will also be extracted. Continuous outcome; SMD across different HRQOL instruments**.** **

**Period: The longest follow up**

---

**3.3.2 Secondary outcomes**

**1.** **Failure to oxygenate**

**Definition:** **Failure to oxygenate** **is** **determined** **if the modality was unable to sustain an oxygen saturation > 88% or partial pressure of oxygen > 60 mm Hg despite treatment with 100% FiO2 and** **optimal** **manipulations of flow rate and airway pressure**

**Period: until** **72 hours**

---

**2. Failure to ventilate**

**Definition: Failure to ventilate is** **determined** **if patients** **remain** **acutely hypercapnic and** **acidemic** **with lack of reduction in pCO2 or improvement in pH despite** **optimal** **settings per institutional standard** **within 72 hours**. **Regarding** **n**ot applicable cases**,** **p**articipants with respiratory failure without baseline hypercapnia are not included in the denominator for this outcome. Studies enrolling only respiratory failure **patients** **without baseline hypercapnia will contribute no data to this outcome.**

**Period: until** **72** **hours** ** **

**3. All adverse events**

**Definition: definition of adverse events** **is** **set by original authors.**

**Incidence proportion of all adverse events**

**Period: during follow up period**
