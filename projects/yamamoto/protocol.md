## 1.      Background

## Paradoxical

reactions (PRs) are defined as immune-mediated adverse events that occur after
TNFα inhibition and cause cytokine imbalance, which triggers new or worsened
inflammatory diseases(1). TNFα inhibits the
maturation of plasmacytoid dendritic cells (pDCs) and suppresses interferon
(IFN) production. TNFα blockade induces excessive IFNα production, which
activates T cells and promotes their migration to the skin and other inflamed
tissues, thereby causing paradoxical inflammation (2,3).
Paradoxical skin lesions include psoriasiform PRs, which are the most
frequent, while lupus-like and sarcoidosis-like PRs occur less often (4).

TNFα inhibitors account for most reported PRs (91.2%),
followed by IL-17 inhibitors (3.5%), IL-4 inhibitors (2.7%), IL-12/23
inhibitors (2.4%), and IL-23 inhibitors (0.01%) (4).
Conventional synthetic disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (csDMARDs), such
as methotrexate (MTX), have also been associated with PRs. However, the
incidence rate ratio (IRR) of PRs ranges from 2.0 to 5.94 in patients treated
with TNFα inhibitors compared with those who received csDMARDs (5), indicating a lower frequency of PRs with
csDMARDs.

No specific effective treatment has been established for
patients who develop PRs. In patients with rheumatoid arthritis who develop
PRs, the need to treat both the skin symptoms caused by PRs and the joint
symptoms associated with the underlying disease makes treatment difficult (2). Continuation of biologics is not
contraindicated after PRs, but resumption of treatment with the same type of
Bio or a related class of Bio has also been shown to be a risk for skin PR
relapse. If biologics are discontinued because skin symptoms due to PRs do not
improve, exacerbation of joint symptoms due to rheumatoid arthritis can be
expected. There are no guidelines for the treatment of patients with
paradoxical reactions with reduction or discontinuation of biologic agents, and
each case should be evaluated by a rheumatologist (6).

The systematic review and meta-analysis (SR&MA) by
Murphy MJ et al., published in 2020, examined cutaneous PRs following biologic
therapy (4). The authors reviewed 313 articles
comprising 2,049 PR cases published up to August 11, 2020. The analysis
reported the distribution of causative biologic agents, types of cutaneous PR
manifestations, and the proportion of cases with symptom improvement.

However, the study did not evaluate treatment strategies for
the underlying disease or the clinical course of joint symptoms after PR onset.

## 2.      Research Question

Management of Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) in Patients Who
Develop Paradoxical Reactions (PRs) During Biologic Therapy

1. What therapeutic options are
   available for controlling arthritis after the onset of PRs?
2. Should the biologic agent be
   switched to a different class following the development of PRs?
3. How does RA disease activity
   change after the occurrence of PRs?

Method

Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses Extension for Scoping Reviews)(7)guidelines and adopts the five-stage
methodological framework developed by the Joanna Briggs Institute(8).

**4.2. Stage 2: Identification of Relevant Research
(Eligibility Criteria)**

Inclusion criteriawill
be defined using the Population, Concept, and Context (PCC) framework, as

**4.2.1 Participants**

Target Diseases:

●       Rheumatoid arthritis (RA)

●       systemic-onset juvenile idiopathic
arthritis (sJIA)

●       adult-onset Still's disease (AOSD)

Patients with PRs who developed PRs while using biologics
and JAK inhibitors in the above diseases.

The definition of RA will be based on the 2010 ACR/EULAR
classification criteria (9) or the 1987 ACR classification criteria(10) or will
include cases diagnosed as RA by the authors.

For pediatric rheumatoid arthritis,  JIA cases will be included if they are
defined according to the 2001 ILAR classification criteria (11) and meet the
criteria for sJIA, or if they are diagnosed as sJIA by the authors.

AOSD will be included because the 2023 joint EULAR (European
League Against Rheumatism)/PReS (Pediatric Rheumatology Society)
recommendations clearly state that sJIA and AOSD represent the same disease
entity and differ only by age at onset(12).

**4.2.2
Concept**

Patients will be eligible for inclusion if paradoxical reactions (PRs) develop
during treatment with biologic agents or Janus kinase inhibitors(JAKi), which
constitute targeted synthetic DMARDs, for any of the target diseases.

For study selection, PRs will be operationally defined as
immune-mediated events presenting as either

●       New-onset immune-mediated
conditions, or

●       Exacerbations of pre-existing
immune-mediated diseases

occurring after initiation of biologic or JAKi therapy and
described by the original authors as paradoxical, treatment-emergent,
drug-induced, or immune-mediated.

We will include biologic DMARDs or JAKi used in RA, listed
in a previous review(13).

Peficitinib, a pan-JAKi approved exclusively in Japan since
2019, will be included and is characterised by moderate inhibition of JAK3 and
mild, broad inhibition of JAK1, JAK2, and leukocyte receptor tyrosine kinase2
(TYK2)(14)**.**

PRs reported during on-label or off-label use will be
included, as will reactions associated with both originator products and
biosimilars, where applicable.

List of biologic DMARDs (bDMARDs):

Patients whose underlying immune-mediated disease will also
be eligible if disease worsening occurs after administration of bDMARDs or JAKi
and is interpreted by the original authors as paradoxical or immune-mediated
rather than attributable to natural disease progression or infection.

**4.2.3 Context**

1. **Place of Diagnosis:** Both inpatient and outpatient
   settings will be included.
2. **Diagnosing Physician:** Diagnoses made by
   dermatologists or internal medicine specialists were accepted.
3. **Timing of Diagnosis:** No restrictions were placed on
   the timing of diagnosis.
4. **Eligible
   Articles:**
   Case reports, case series, narrative reviews, and literature reviews
   related to the aforementioned diseases were included. Conference abstracts
   and presentations were excluded.

**4.2.4 Types
of Research**

This scoping review will include experimental and
quasi-experimental designs, such as randomized controlled trials,
non-randomized controlled trials, and before-and-after studies. Observational
studies, including prospective and retrospective cohort studies, case-control
studies, and cross-sectional studies, will also be considered. Descriptive
observational designs, such as case series, individual case reports, and
descriptive cross-sectional studies, will be included. Conference abstracts
will be excluded.

## 4.3. Stage 3: Study selection

Two independent reviewers will screen titles and abstracts. All studies
selected by either reviewer will advance to full-text review. Each reviewer
will then independently assess full texts for eligibility. Discrepancies
between reviewers will be resolved through discussion or, if necessary,
consultation with a third reviewer. The search results and study selection
process will be fully documented in the final scoping review and presented
using the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses
extension for Scoping Reviews (PRISMA-ScR) flow diagram.

**4.4
Stage 4: Charting the data**

Two
independent reviewers will extract data separately. Extracted data will include
specific details on participants, concepts, context, study design, and key
findings related to the review question. Data extraction will cover the
following items:

●
Age

●
Sex

●
PR subtype: psoriasis / uveitis /
sarcoid-like / lupus / IBD-like / others

●
Biologic agents or JAKi used before
and after PR onset

●
PR resolution rate: complete
remission / partial remission / no improvement

●
RA treatment response after PR
onset: remission/ exacerbation / not reported

●       Therapeutic strategies used to
control arthritis afterPR onset: continuation/ discontinuation/ switching of
bDMARDs or JAKi/ initiation or intensification of csDMARDs

●
Use of csDMARDs without biologic or JAK inhibitors after PR
onset, where reported

Following data extraction, changes
in therapeutic decisions before and after PR onset will be evaluated, including
proportions of Stop (%), Continue (%), Switch within same class (%), and Switch
to other class (%).

Discrepancies between the two
reviewers will be resolved through discussion. If necessary, a third reviewer
will be consulted. Original study authors will be contacted when clarification
is required. The data extraction form will be pretested using a random sample
of ten studies.
